Game Overview

Dual Mastery is a tactical card game where players combine two different fighting styles to create a unique deck. Players use stance-based combat, resource management, and strategic planning to reduce their opponent's health to zero. The Bloodline Legacy variant adds a generational element where fighting styles evolve through succession.

🎯 Players

2 players in competitive duels, or cooperative trials against automated enemies

⏱️ Play Time

15-30 minutes per duel, with ongoing bloodline campaigns

🎲 Age

12+ (tactical complexity and thematic content)

⚔️ Complexity

Medium-High strategic depth with accessible core mechanics

Setup & Components

Components

  • 6+ style decks (8 cards each)
  • Legacy cards for each style (3 cards each)
  • Focus dice (d4, d6, d8, d10, d12)
  • Health trackers
  • Focus tokens
  • Range tracking marker
  • Bloodline Legacy tracking sheet

Game Setup

  1. Each player selects 2 fighting styles (8 cards each) and combines them into a 16-card deck
  2. Shuffle your deck and draw 5 cards to form your starting hand
  3. Each player starts with 20 health
  4. Determine the first player randomly
  5. Set the Range Track to show both players at Close Range initially

Game Area

  • Hand: Cards you can play
  • Focus Area: Cards placed face-down as resources
  • Stance Area: Your active stance card (if any)
  • Meditation Zone: Discard pile
  • Range Track: Token showing relative positioning (Close or Far Range)
  • Bloodline Record: Tracks style inheritance and mastery across generations

Turn Sequence

1. Beginning Phase

Draw a card (maximum hand size is 7)

2. Focus Phase

You may place any number of cards from your hand face-down in your Focus area. Each Focus card has an associated die (d4, d6, d8, d10, d12).

3. Action Phase

  • Roll all your Focus dice to generate energy
  • Spend energy to play cards from your hand
  • You may change range once by spending 1 energy (unless modified by card effects)
  • Apply any stance advantages/disadvantages and range modifiers
  • Resolve card effects

4. End Phase

  • Used Focus cards go to your Meditation Zone
  • You may bank up to 2 unused energy as "Focused Energy" for your next turn
  • Resolve any "end of turn" effects
  • Play passes to your opponent

Winning Conditions

  • Reduce your opponent's health to 0
  • If a player cannot draw a card when required, they lose

Core Mechanics

Focus System

Energy Generation: Cards placed face-down as Focus generate energy when their associated dice are rolled.

Banking Energy: Up to 2 unused energy can be saved as "Focused Energy" for next turn.

Used Focus: Goes to Meditation (discard pile) after use.

Stance Advantage System

Rock-paper-scissors relationship between three stance types:

  • Aggressive (Red) beats Evasive (Green)
  • Defensive (Blue) beats Aggressive (Red)
  • Evasive (Green) beats Defensive (Blue)

Advantage: Deal +1 damage when attacking

Disadvantage: Opponent draws a card

Range System

Two positions: Close Range and Far Range

  • Close Range Specialists: +1 damage at Close Range
  • Far Range Specialists: +1 damage at Far Range
  • Range Changes: Usually cost 1 energy unless modified

Mastery System

Master Effects: Trigger when you've played 2+ cards of the same style in a turn

Creates strategic tension between specialization and versatility

Bloodline Legacy System

Generational Inheritance

  • Victory: Winner becomes "Champion" and retains both fighting styles
  • Defeat: Create an "Heir" who inherits one style and adopts a new one
  • Challenge: Heirs challenge Champions to reclaim family honor

Style Mastery

Third Generation: When a style is passed down for three consecutive generations, it becomes "Mastered"

Legacy Cards: When a style becomes Mastered, the player may choose any one standard card from that style to replace with any one Legacy Card from that style's available Legacy Cards

Inheritance Path: A style can become Mastered whether you win or lose duels, as long as it's consistently passed down through three generations

Power & Cost: Legacy Cards have powerful effects but meaningful drawbacks representing age

Deck Limit: Only one Legacy Card per Mastered style may be in your deck at a time

Champion's Burden

After winning 3 consecutive matches, Champions face additional challenges:

  • Start with -2 health in subsequent matches
  • Draw one fewer card in starting hand
  • Challenger may view one card in Champion's hand at start

Bloodline Record

Track your family's journey across generations:

  • Style inheritance patterns
  • Mastered fighting styles
  • Memorable duels and legendary moments
  • Family achievements and honors

Legacy Card & Style Inheritance FAQ

Q: Can I gain Legacy Cards even if I lose duels?
A: Yes! Legacy Cards are gained when a style becomes "Mastered" after being passed down for three consecutive generations, regardless of win/loss record. A family can perfect their ancestral techniques through practice and inheritance, not just victory.

Q: When exactly do I replace a card with a Legacy Card?
A: When a style becomes Mastered (3rd generation inheritance), you may immediately choose any standard card from that style to replace with any Legacy Card from that style's available options. This happens during deck construction between duels.

Q: Who chooses which style gets passed down to an Heir?
A: The defeated player chooses which of their two fighting styles to pass down to their Heir.
